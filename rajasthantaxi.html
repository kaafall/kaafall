Explore Rajasthan

Top Destinations

01

Shekhawati

Shekhawati is an open-air art gallery spread across the semi-arid land of northeast Rajasthan. Known as the "Paris of Rajasthan," this region is famous for its intricately painted havelis adorned with vibrant frescoes depicting mythology, daily life, and historical events. The architecture tells the story of wealthy merchant families who once ruled this region, making it a must-visit for art and history lovers.

🎨 Heritage & Art
02

Mandawa

Mandawa is a charming small town in the Shekhawati region, famous for its magnificent medieval fort and centuries-old havelis. The Mandawa Fort, now converted into a heritage hotel, offers panoramic views of the town. The narrow lanes are alive with colorful wall paintings, traditional bazaars, and the smell of street food — a perfect blend of history and local culture.

🏰 Fort Town
03

Nawalgarh

Nawalgarh is one of the finest examples of Shekhawati's painted town heritage. The town is dotted with over 2,000 beautiful havelis, each featuring exquisite fresco paintings. The Morarka Haveli Museum and Poddar Haveli are absolute highlights. Nawalgarh is also known for its vibrant local markets, traditional sweets, and the warmth of Marwari hospitality.

🖼️ Fresco Capital
04

Bikaner

Bikaner, the "Camel Country," is a royal desert city known for its magnificent Junagarh Fort, one of the few forts in Rajasthan never conquered by enemies. The city is also famous for the quirky Karni Mata Temple (Rat Temple), the stunning Lalgarh Palace, and of course — its world-famous Bikaner Bhujia. Camel breeding farms here offer unique experiences for visitors.

🐪 Desert Royalty
05

Jaisalmer – Desert Safari

The golden dunes of Sam and Khuri offer one of India's most breathtaking experiences — a Jaisalmer Desert Safari. Ride camels across endless sand dunes as the sun sets painting the sky in shades of orange and purple. Enjoy a royal dinner under the stars, folk music performances, and a night stay in luxury desert camps. This is Rajasthan at its most magical and unforgettable.

🌅 Dune Experience
06

Jaisalmer

Rising like a mirage from the Thar Desert, the "Golden City" of Jaisalmer is one of India's most iconic destinations. The magnificent Jaisalmer Fort — a living fort where thousands still reside — is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Explore the intricate Patwon Ki Haveli, lose yourself in labyrinthine bazaars selling camel leather goods, and watch the golden sandstone glow at sunset.

🏯 Golden City
07

Jodhpur

The "Blue City" of Jodhpur is a feast for the eyes — thousands of indigo-painted houses cluster around the base of the mighty Mehrangarh Fort. One of India's largest forts, Mehrangarh towers 400 feet above the city offering spectacular views. Visit the Jaswant Thada cenotaph, explore the chaotic Sardar Market, and don't miss the city's vibrant street food scene famous for makhania lassi and mirchi bada.

💙 Blue City
08

Ranakpur

Nestled deep in the Aravalli hills, the Ranakpur Jain Temple is one of India's most spectacular pieces of architecture. Built in the 15th century, this white marble temple features 1,444 intricately carved pillars — no two alike. Surrounded by dense forests, the temple complex is not just a religious site but an architectural marvel that leaves every visitor in absolute awe of ancient Indian craftsmanship.

🕌 Marble Temple
09

Udaipur

Udaipur, the "City of Lakes," is arguably Rajasthan's most romantic destination. The shimmering Lake Pichola, with its legendary Lake Palace hotel floating like a dream in the middle, defines the city's charm. Explore the grand City Palace complex, wander through the Jagdish Temple bazaar, watch the sunset from Sajjangarh (Monsoon Palace), and lose yourself in the city's fairy-tale atmosphere that has inspired poets, painters, and filmmakers alike.

🏝️ City of Lakes
10

Bundi

Bundi is Rajasthan's best-kept secret — a stunning step-well city that feels frozen in time. The Taragarh Fort, perched dramatically on a hilltop, offers mesmerizing views of the town below. The Bundi Palace is famous for its exceptional murals and intricate paintings. The ancient stepwells (baoris) of Bundi are architectural marvels, and the old town with its blue-washed houses rivals even Jodhpur in charm.

🌿 Hidden Gem
11

Pushkar

Pushkar is one of India's oldest and most sacred towns, built around the holy Pushkar Lake. Home to the world's only Brahma Temple, this spiritual town has a magical energy that draws both pilgrims and backpackers. The famous Pushkar Camel Fair (held every November) is Asia's largest livestock fair and a UNESCO-recognised cultural event. The sunset view from the Savitri Temple hilltop is one you'll never forget.

🪔 Sacred Town
12

Jaipur

The "Pink City" of Jaipur is Rajasthan's vibrant capital — a perfect blend of royal heritage and modern life. The iconic Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds), the majestic Amber Fort, the City Palace, and the Jantar Mantar observatory make Jaipur a UNESCO World Heritage city. Shop for gems, block-print textiles, and blue pottery in the colorful bazaars of Johari Bazar and Tripolia Bazar. Jaipur is every traveller's dream.

🌸 Pink City
13

Ranthambore

Ranthambore National Park is one of India's best places to spot the Royal Bengal Tiger in its natural habitat. Once a royal hunting ground of the Jaipur Maharajas, today this vast sanctuary is a conservation success story. The dramatic ancient Ranthambore Fort sits within the park boundaries, creating a unique backdrop of wildlife and heritage. Zone safaris in early morning light offer the best chance of tiger sightings.

🐯 Tiger Country
