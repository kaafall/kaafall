Day-by-Day Plan

2N 3D Mukteshwar Itinerary

Your Mukteshwar journey begins the moment we pick you up from Delhi. A comfortable drive through Rampur, Rudrapur and Bhowali brings you into Kumaon's quiet orchard country — a small ridge-top village known for its 350-year-old Shiva temple, dramatic cliff-edge viewpoints, and rows of apple and apricot orchards. Over the next two days you'll stand at Chauli ki Jali with the Himalayas stretched out below, walk through the orchards of Ramgarh, chase a hidden waterfall, and simply breathe the mountain quiet. Every detail is handled — you just need to show up.

Frequently Asked Questions

Mukteshwar is a small hill town in the Nainital district of Uttarakhand, situated at an altitude of about 2,285 meters (7,500 ft). It's known for its cliff-edge Himalayan views and centuries-old temple.
Mukteshwar is approximately 350 km from Delhi by road. The journey takes around 8–9 hours via Rampur, Rudrapur and Bhowali — comfortably done in a single day.
By road: Delhi → Rampur → Rudrapur → Bhowali → Mukteshwar. Kafall provides private cab transfers directly from Delhi. By rail: Take a train to Kathgodam, then a cab to Mukteshwar (~50 km).
March–June offers pleasant weather with blooming orchards, perfect for walks and clear Himalayan views. September–November brings the clearest post-monsoon skies. December–February is chilly with occasional snowfall, ideal for a cosy cottage stay by the fireplace.
Mukteshwar is famous for the 350-year-old Shiva temple, the dramatic cliff-edge viewpoint of Chauli ki Jali, panoramic Himalayan peaks visible on clear days, apple and apricot orchards, and as a growing spot for paragliding.
Yes — most of Mukteshwar's highlights (temple, viewpoint, orchards) involve gentle walks rather than strenuous treks, making it well suited for families, older travellers, and anyone looking for a relaxed hill getaway.
Visiting the Mukteshwar Temple, standing at the Chauli ki Jali cliff viewpoint, walking through Ramgarh's apple orchards, a short trek to Bhalu Gaad Waterfall, paragliding (seasonal), and quiet evenings by a bonfire.
Yes, Mukteshwar occasionally receives light snowfall in January–February, adding to its quiet, misty charm. However, snow isn't guaranteed every year, so it's best treated as a bonus rather than the main draw.
