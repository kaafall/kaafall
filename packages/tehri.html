Day-by-Day Plan

3N 4D Tehri Lake Itinerary

Your Tehri Lake journey unfolds across four unhurried days. A scenic drive through Roorkee, Haridwar, Rishikesh and Chamba brings you to the edge of Asia's largest man-made lake — a sapphire reservoir where the old town of Tehri once stood, now cradled by pine-covered Himalayan slopes. Over the next three days you'll race across the water on a jet ski, walk the swaying Dobra-Chanti Suspension Bridge, take in the Tehri Dam from its viewpoint, and fall asleep to lakeside bonfires under a wide open sky. Every detail — cab, stay, water sports permits — is handled, so you just need to show up.

Frequently Asked Questions

Tehri Lake is in Tehri Garhwal district, Uttarakhand, formed by the Tehri Dam on the Bhagirathi and Bhilangna rivers. It submerged the old town of Tehri, which was relocated and rebuilt as New Tehri nearby.
Tehri Lake is approximately 300–320 km from Delhi by road, and the drive takes around 7–9 hours via Meerut, Roorkee, Haridwar, Rishikesh and Chamba.
By road: Delhi → Meerut → Roorkee → Haridwar → Rishikesh → Chamba → Koti Colony jetty. Kafall provides private cab transfers directly from Delhi. By rail: Take a train to Rishikesh, then a cab to Tehri Lake (~85–110 km). By air: Fly into Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun, then a scenic drive of about 3 hours.
March–June and September–November offer the most pleasant weather for water sports and sightseeing. The Tehri Lake Festival, celebrating adventure sports and local culture, is usually held in February. Winters (December–February) are cool and quieter, with limited water sports operations.
Jet skiing, speed boating, banana boat rides, kayaking, rowing, knee boarding and parasailing are the most popular activities, run by certified operators at the Tehri Lake Adventure Sports Complex near Koti Colony.
Open-water swimming is not recommended due to the lake's depth and safety concerns. Water-based recreation is only permitted within designated, supervised activity zones with trained operators.
Yes — gentler options like boating, kayaking and lakeside sightseeing suit families and older travellers well, while jet skiing and parasailing cater to those wanting more of a thrill. Most high-energy activities have age and health restrictions, so check with your trip manager.
The Tehri Dam viewpoint, the Dobra-Chanti Suspension Bridge, New Tehri town, Koti Colony, Chamba, Devprayag's river confluence, and Kanatal with the Surkanda Devi Temple are the key sightseeing spots — most are covered in our Standard and Premium packages.
Yes — from simple homestays in Chamba (₹1,500/night) to premium boutique lakeside camps with panoramic views (₹6,800+/night). Kafall partners only with verified, quality-checked properties.
The lake area itself rarely sees snow, but nearby higher points like Kanatal and Surkanda Devi Temple occasionally get light winter snowfall — a nice add-on if you extend your itinerary.
