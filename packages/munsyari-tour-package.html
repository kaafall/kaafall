Day-by-Day Plan

4N 5D Munsyari Itinerary

Your Munsyari journey begins the moment we pick you up from Delhi. Given the distance, we split the drive with an overnight halt at scenic Kausani before continuing to Munsyari — the last motorable town in the Johar Valley and gateway to the Milam Glacier. Over the following days you'll trek to the snow-covered Khaliya Top bugyal for uninterrupted views of the five Panchachuli peaks, visit the cascading Birthi Falls, explore the Tribal Heritage Museum, and soak in a landscape that remains refreshingly free of crowds. Every detail is handled — you just need to show up.
1

Delhi → Kausani (Halt)

Departure · Scenic Drive · Overnight Stop

  • Early morning pickup from Delhi (5–6 AM)
  • Drive via Rudrapur → Kathgodam → Almora
  • Breakfast & lunch stops en route
  • Arrive Kausani by evening; check-in
  • Sunset views of the Himalayan range from Kausani
  • Dinner & overnight stay to break the long drive
2

Kausani → Munsyari via Birthi Falls

Berinag · Thal · Waterfall Stop · Arrival

  • Early breakfast; drive via Berinag & Thal
  • Photo stop at Birthi Falls en route
  • Continue through pine and oak forests
  • Arrive Munsyari by evening; check-in
  • First glimpse of the Panchachuli peaks at sunset
  • Dinner and rest at guesthouse
3

Khaliya Top Trek

Snow Bugyal · Panchachuli Panorama

  • Early start with packed breakfast
  • Trek to Khaliya Top (3,500m) — moderate 9 km trail
  • Alpine meadows, oak forests & snow patches en route
  • 360° views of the five Panchachuli peaks
  • Packed lunch at the top; descend by afternoon
  • Return to Munsyari; bonfire dinner
4

Munsyari Local Sightseeing

Nanda Devi Viewpoint · Tribal Museum · Thamri Kund

  • Post-breakfast visit to Nanda Devi viewpoint
  • Explore the Tribal Heritage Museum at Ral village
  • Short walk to Thamri Kund forest lake
  • Visit a local Shauka village to learn about Johar culture
  • Evening free for shopping local woollens & shawls
  • Farewell bonfire dinner under the stars
5

Munsyari → Delhi Departure

Check-out · Long Scenic Drive · Home

  • Early breakfast & guesthouse check-out
  • Long drive back via Thal, Berinag & Almora
  • Dinner stop en route (on request)
  • Drop back at Delhi late night / early next morning
  • Memories of the untouched Himalaya packed, soul refreshed 🏔️

Frequently Asked Questions

Munsyari is a small town in the Pithoragarh district of Uttarakhand, situated at an altitude of about 2,200 meters (7,217 ft) in the Johar Valley. It is the last major motorable town before the Milam Glacier and higher Himalayan region.
Munsyari is approximately 640 km from Delhi by road. The journey takes around 14–16 hours, which is why we recommend splitting it with an overnight halt at Kausani or Almora.
By road: Delhi → Kathgodam → Almora → Berinag → Thal → Munsyari. Kafall provides private cab transfers from Delhi with an overnight halt. By train: Take a train to Kathgodam, then a cab to Munsyari (~275 km).
April–June offers clear skies and blooming rhododendrons, ideal for peak views. September–November brings crisp, crystal-clear views of the Panchachuli range post-monsoon. December–February brings snowfall — beautiful but roads can be affected.
Munsyari is famous for uninterrupted views of the five Panchachuli peaks, the Khaliya Top snow meadow trek, and as the gateway to the Johar Valley and Milam Glacier trekking region. It's one of Uttarakhand's least crowded Himalayan destinations.
The Khaliya Top trek (9 km) is moderate and best suited to reasonably fit travellers. Local sightseeing — Nanda Devi viewpoint, Birthi Falls, Tribal Museum, Thamri Kund — is easy and suitable for families and older travellers.
Trekking to Khaliya Top, visiting Birthi Falls and Thamri Kund, exploring the Tribal Heritage Museum, village walks through Shauka settlements, photography of the Panchachuli range, stargazing, and bonfire evenings.
Yes! From December to February, Munsyari and the surrounding Khaliya Top region receive good snowfall. However, road conditions from Almora onward can be affected, so we recommend the standard or premium plan with flexible scheduling in winter.
Yes — from simple homestays (₹1,600/night) to premium guesthouses with direct Panchachuli views (₹5,000+/night). Facilities are basic compared to popular hill stations, which is exactly what keeps Munsyari peaceful. Kafall partners only with verified, quality-checked properties.
No — Munsyari remains one of Uttarakhand's most offbeat destinations. Being far from Delhi and less commercialised, it sees a fraction of the tourist footfall of places like Nainital or Mussoorie, keeping the valley pristine and quiet.
