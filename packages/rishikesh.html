Day-by-Day Plan

2N 3D Rishikesh Itinerary

Your Rishikesh trip mixes adrenaline with calm. Over 3 days you'll paddle through the Ganga's rapids, walk the iconic jhulas, sit for the evening Ganga aarti, ease into a sunrise yoga session, and chase a hidden waterfall — all without an overnight drive, since Rishikesh is a comfortable day's journey from Delhi.
1

Delhi → Rishikesh

Morning Departure · Arrival · Ganga Aarti

  • Early morning pickup from Delhi (5–6 AM)
  • Drive via Meerut, Roorkee & Haridwar
  • Arrive Rishikesh by afternoon; hotel/camp check-in
  • Lunch & rest, followed by Laxman Jhula & Ram Jhula walk
  • Café hopping along the Ganga (German Bakery lanes)
  • Evening Ganga aarti at Triveni/Parmarth Ghat
2

Rafting Day + Camp Bonfire

White-water Rafting · Cliff Jumping · Bonfire

  • Breakfast at camp; safety briefing by 9 AM
  • 16 km rafting from Shivpuri through Grade II–III rapids
  • Optional cliff jumping & body surfing stops en route
  • Lunch break by the riverside
  • Free evening for beach games / river-side relaxation
  • Bonfire & dinner at camp under the stars
3

Yoga Morning + Waterfall

Sunrise Yoga · Neer Garh Falls · Departure

  • Guided sunrise yoga & meditation session by the Ganga
  • Breakfast & hotel check-out
  • Short trek to Neer Garh waterfall for a dip
  • Optional visit to Neelkanth Mahadev temple
  • Lunch stop, then drive back towards Delhi
  • Drop back at Delhi by evening (7–9 PM) 🙏

Frequently Asked Questions

Rishikesh is around 240 km from Delhi by road, taking about 5–6 hours. It's a comfortable day drive, so no overnight travel is needed to reach.
Yes, the standard 16 km stretch from Shivpuri to Rishikesh has Grade II–III rapids, suitable for first-timers. Certified guides, life jackets and helmets are provided, along with a safety briefing before the ride.
Rafting season runs from mid-September to June. It's usually closed during the monsoon (July–mid September) due to high water levels and safety concerns.
The Ganga aarti at Triveni Ghat and Parmarth Niketan typically begins around sunset, roughly 6–7 PM depending on the season. It's a beautiful, peaceful ceremony worth planning your evening around.
Rishikesh is a holy town where alcohol and non-vegetarian food are officially banned/restricted within municipal limits. Most stays and restaurants serve pure vegetarian food only — do keep this in mind while planning.
Not at all! Our guided sessions are beginner-friendly, led by certified instructors, and focus on gentle stretches, breathing (pranayama) and short meditation — perfect for first-timers.
Yes, Rishikesh suits everyone — families enjoy the calmer sightseeing and aarti, couples love the riverside cafés and cottages, and groups love the rafting and camp bonfires.
Absolutely. While rafting requires basic fitness, seniors can enjoy the Ganga aarti, jhula walks, café time, yoga sessions and sightseeing without any strain. Rafting can be skipped or swapped for a shorter, gentler stretch.
Bungee jumping, flying fox, giant swing, cliff jumping, kayaking and camping are all available as add-ons in Rishikesh, bookable separately through Kafall.
Yes, Kafall only partners with verified, well-reviewed camps and hotels, and our trip manager stays reachable throughout for any support solo travellers may need.
