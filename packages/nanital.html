Day-by-Day Plan

3N 4D Nainital Itinerary

Your Nainital journey begins the moment we pick you up from Delhi. Over 4 days you'll drive through Moradabad and Rampur into the Kumaon hills, settle beside the emerald Naini Lake, ride the cable car up to Snow View Point for a first glimpse of the Himalayan skyline, seek blessings at the Naina Devi Temple, and spend a day among the twin lakes of Bhimtal and Sattal. Every detail is handled — you just need to show up.
1

Delhi → Moradabad → Nainital

Departure · Scenic Drive · Lakeside Arrival

  • Morning pickup from Delhi (6–8 AM)
  • Drive via Moradabad → Rampur → Rudrapur → Haldwani
  • Breakfast stop en route
  • Arrive Nainital by afternoon (2–4 PM)
  • Hotel check-in, freshen up & rest
  • Evening stroll on Mall Road, Naini Lake boating
2

Naina Devi Temple + Snow View Point

Sacred Shrine · Cable Car · Himalayan Panorama

  • Morning darshan at Naina Devi Temple, Naini Lake's north shore
  • Ropeway ride up to Snow View Point
  • Views of Nanda Devi, Trishul & the Kumaon Himalayas
  • Optional Naina Peak (China Peak) pony ride
  • Afternoon visit to Eco Cave Gardens
  • Evening leisure & shopping at Tibetan Market
3

Bhimtal + Sattal Day Trip

Twin Lakes · Island Aquarium · Birdwatching

  • Early breakfast; drive to Bhimtal (22 km)
  • Boating at Bhimtal Lake, visit the island aquarium
  • Continue to Sattal — seven interconnected freshwater lakes
  • Birdwatching amid pine and oak forest
  • Return to Nainital by evening; sunset at Tiffin Top / Land's End
  • Dinner & leisure evening
4

Nainital → Delhi Departure

Check-out · Scenic Drive · Home

  • Breakfast & hotel check-out
  • Optional last-minute shopping on Mall Road
  • Drive back via Haldwani, Rampur & Moradabad
  • Optional stop at Kainchi Dham en route (subject to time)
  • Drop back at Delhi by evening (8–9 PM)
  • Memories of the lakes packed, soul refreshed 🏞️

Frequently Asked Questions

Nainital is a hill station in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, situated at an altitude of about 2,084 meters (6,837 ft) around the crescent-shaped Naini Lake. It is the district headquarters of Nainital district.
Nainital is approximately 320 km from Delhi by road. The journey takes around 7–8 hours. Most guests prefer a morning departure from Delhi to arrive by afternoon.
By road: Delhi → Moradabad → Rampur → Rudrapur → Haldwani → Nainital. Kafall provides private cab transfers from Delhi. By rail: Take a train to Kathgodam (the nearest railhead), then a cab to Nainital (~35 km, about an hour).
Nainital is pleasant year-round. Summer (March–June) offers cool weather and clear skies, ideal to escape the plains' heat. Monsoon (July–Sept) brings lush greenery but occasional landslides. Winter (Dec–Feb) can bring snowfall — magical for snow lovers.
Nainital is famous as the "Lake District of India" for Naini Lake and its many surrounding lakes — Bhimtal, Sattal, Naukuchiatal and Khurpatal. It's also known for Naina Devi Temple, the Snow View Point ropeway, colonial-era architecture and Mall Road.
Yes! Nainital is one of the most family-friendly hill stations — boating on Naini Lake, the ropeway to Snow View Point, and gentle walks around Mall Road need no trekking experience at all. It's equally great for couples, families with kids and senior travellers.
Boating on Naini Lake, the Snow View Point ropeway, Naina Devi Temple darshan, shopping at Tibetan Market and Mall Road, day trips to Bhimtal and Sattal, birdwatching, horse riding to Naina Peak, and sunset views from Tiffin Top or Land's End.
Yes, Nainital occasionally receives snowfall between December and February, though it's less frequent and heavy than higher hill stations. When it does snow, Naini Lake's surroundings and the Mall Road turn into a picture-postcard scene.
Yes — from budget guesthouses near Mall Road (₹1,800/night) to premium heritage resorts with lake views (₹6,000+/night). Kafall partners only with verified, quality-checked properties across every budget.
Nainital does get busy during summer holidays and long weekends, especially around Mall Road and the lake. Kafall plans sightseeing timings to help you avoid the worst of the crowds, and the nearby lakes of Bhimtal and Sattal offer a quieter alternative.
