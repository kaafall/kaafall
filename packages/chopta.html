Day-by-Day Plan

3N 4D Chopta Tungnath Itinerary

Your Chopta journey starts the moment we pick you up from Delhi. Over 4 days you'll drive through the Rishikesh-Rudraprayag valley, trek up to the world's highest Shiva temple, summit Chandrashila for a 360° Himalayan panorama, and walk beside the mirror-still waters of Deoria Tal. We've planned every detail — all you need to do is show up.
1

Delhi → Rudraprayag → Chopta

Departure · Overnight Drive · Arrival

  • Evening pickup from Delhi (7–9 PM)
  • Overnight drive via Rishikesh & Rudraprayag
  • Breakfast stop en route with river views
  • Arrive Chopta by afternoon (2–3 PM)
  • Camp/homestay check-in, freshen up & rest
  • Evening acclimatisation walk in the meadows
2

Tungnath Temple + Chandrashila Summit

Sunrise Trek · World's Highest Shiva Temple

  • Early start (4–5 AM) for the sunrise trek
  • Trek to Tungnath Temple (3.5 km, 12,073 ft)
  • Continue to Chandrashila summit (~1.5 km further)
  • Sunrise views of Nanda Devi, Trishul & Kedarnath peaks
  • Descend & lunch back at Chopta
  • Bonfire & dinner at camp
3

Deoria Tal + Rohini Bugyal

Mirror Lake Trek · Alpine Meadows

  • Breakfast at camp; drive to Sari village
  • Trek to Deoria Tal (2 km) — Chaukhamba reflection lake
  • Lakeside photography & picnic lunch
  • Visit Rohini Bugyal alpine meadows
  • Rhododendron forest nature walk
  • Return to Chopta; bonfire evening
4

Chopta → Delhi Departure

Check-out · Scenic Drive · Home

  • Early breakfast & camp check-out
  • Quick stop at Ukhimath viewpoint
  • Drive back through Rudraprayag & Rishikesh
  • Optional Rishikesh riverside stop
  • Drop back at Delhi late evening (9–10 PM)
  • Trip memories packed, hearts full 🏔️

Frequently Asked Questions

Chopta is a high-altitude meadow in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand, at around 2,680 meters (8,790 ft), often called the "Mini Switzerland of India." It's the base for the Tungnath Temple and Chandrashila trek.
Chopta is approximately 460 km from Delhi by road via Rishikesh and Rudraprayag. The journey takes around 10–12 hours, so most guests prefer an overnight departure.
By road: Delhi → Rishikesh → Rudraprayag → Ukhimath → Chopta. Kafall arranges private cabs for a comfortable door-to-door journey. By train: Take a train to Rishikesh/Haridwar and then a cab to Chopta.
The Tungnath trek (3.5 km) is easy-to-moderate on a paved path. The extension to Chandrashila summit (further ~1.5 km) is moderately steep. Reasonable fitness is enough; our guides pace the trek for all group members.
Chopta is most famous for Tungnath — the highest Shiva temple in the world — and the Chandrashila summit trek, which offers 360° views of Nanda Devi, Trishul, and Kedarnath peaks. Deoria Tal and its alpine meadows are also major draws.
The Tungnath trek is family-friendly for reasonably fit members, including older kids. The Chandrashila summit push is better suited to adults comfortable with a moderate trek. We customise the itinerary for family groups.
Sunrise trekking to Chandrashila, Tungnath Temple darshan, the Deoria Tal lake trek, birdwatching, nature photography, bonfire evenings, and walks through rhododendron forests and high-altitude bugyals (meadows).
Yes! From December to February, Chopta and the Tungnath trail receive heavy snowfall, making the trek more challenging but visually stunning. Spring (April–June) and autumn (Sept–Nov) are best for regular trekking.
Yes — from budget homestays (₹1,800/night) to premium mountain-view camps and resorts (₹4,800+/night) in and around Chopta and Duggalbitta. Kafall partners only with verified, quality-checked properties.
No special permit is needed for the Tungnath-Chandrashila trek, just a valid government ID. Kafall handles any local entry formalities as part of your package.
