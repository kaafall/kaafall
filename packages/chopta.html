About the Destination

What Makes Chopta So Special?

Chopta is a small, pristine meadow in Uttarakhand's Kedarnath Wildlife Sanctuary, often called the Mini Switzerland of India. Unlike over-commercialised hill stations, Chopta retains its raw, untouched Himalayan soul — dense rhododendron forests, silent alpine meadows, and jaw-dropping snow vistas await every traveller who makes the journey here.

The Chopta Tungnath Chandrashila Trek is one of the most rewarding weekend treks from Delhi. The 4 km trail from Chopta to Tungnath passes through enchanting forests of oak and rhododendron, while the final 1 km push to Chandrashila summit reveals a breathtaking panorama of the Garhwal Himalaya.

Whether you are a solo traveller, a couple seeking a romantic snow honeymoon in Uttarakhand, a family, or a group of adventure-seekers, Chopta offers an experience like no other. From summer blooms to winter snowfall — every season paints a different portrait.

Kafall's Chopta Tour Package covers everything — transportation from Delhi, Haridwar, Rishikesh, or Dehradun; accommodation; guided treks; Dhari Devi darshan; and an unforgettable bonfire night under a star-filled Himalayan sky.

Trek Details

The Three Iconic Treks

From beginner-friendly meadow walks to thrilling summit climbs — Chopta region offers something for every level of trekker.

01

Tungnath Trek

Easy – Moderate

The trail to Tungnath Temple — the world's highest Shiva shrine — is 4 km one-way from Chopta. The well-paved, scenic path passes through dense rhododendron and oak forests and offers stunning views of Kedar Peak and Nanda Devi. Suitable for beginners and families, it typically takes 2–3 hours one-way.

📏 4 km one-way ⏱ 2–3 hrs up 📐 Elevation gain: ~1,000m
02

Chandrashila Trek

Moderate

Continue 1 km beyond Tungnath to reach the Chandrashila Summit at 4,000 m. The final climb is steep but rewarding with a 360° panorama of Nanda Devi, Trishul, Chaukhamba, Kedar Dome, and Bandarpunch. This is a must-do for any trekker visiting Chopta. Best enjoyed at sunrise.

📏 5 km one-way ⏱ 3–4 hrs up 🌄 360° Summit Views
03

Deoria Tal Trek

Easy

The Deoria Tal Trek starts from Sari Village near Ukhimath and leads to a gorgeous high-altitude lake that mirrors the Chaukhamba peaks. The 3 km trail is easy, making it perfect for families and first-time trekkers. Often combined with the Chopta Tungnath package to create a premium 5-day Uttarakhand adventure.

📏 3 km one-way ⏱ 1.5–2 hrs up 🏞 Reflection Lake

Chopta Travel Guide — Everything You Need to Know

Chopta Tungnath Chandrashila Trek Overview

The Chopta Tungnath Chandrashila Trek is widely regarded as one of the best weekend treks in Uttarakhand. Starting from the meadow of Chopta at 2,680 m, the trail ascends through magical forests to Tungnath Temple at 3,680 m — the highest Shiva temple in the world and one of the Panch Kedar shrines. From Tungnath, a short but steep trail leads to the Chandrashila Summit at 4,000 m, where trekkers are rewarded with a sweeping 360° panoramic view of the Himalayas including Nanda Devi (7,816 m), Trishul (7,120 m), and the Chaukhamba massif.

Chopta Snowfall — When Is It and What to Expect?

Chopta snowfall typically begins in late November and peaks through January and February. During this period, the entire meadow, trekking trail, and surrounding forests are blanketed in a thick layer of snow. The Tungnath trail can have 4–6 feet of snow in peak winter, making it an exhilarating and beautiful snow trek. Our winter packages include snow-safe equipment, gaiters, and experienced local guides who know the trail in all conditions.

Is Chopta Safe for Solo Travellers?

Yes, Chopta is one of the safest adventure destinations in India for solo travellers. Kafall's group departures every Friday from Delhi are especially popular with solo trekkers — you'll join a small group of 10–20 like-minded travellers, guided by our experienced trek leaders. The community atmosphere, set itinerary, and 24/7 support make it an ideal first Himalayan adventure for solo travellers from cities like Delhi, Noida, Gurgaon, Jaipur, and Lucknow.

Chopta vs Auli — Which Should You Choose?

Auli is India's premier ski destination with gondola rides and luxury resorts, while Chopta is a raw, authentic Himalayan meadow perfect for trekking, camping, and spiritual exploration. Chopta is less commercial, more affordable, and offers a far more immersive mountain experience. For those seeking adventure trekking + temple visits + snowfall + camping — Chopta wins decisively. For skiing and resort holidays, Auli is the better choice.
