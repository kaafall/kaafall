Day-by-Day Plan

3N 4D Chopta Itinerary

Your Chopta journey begins the moment we pick you up from Delhi. Over 4 days you'll drive through the spiritual town of Haridwar, stay in the serene meadows of Chopta, trek to the world's highest Shiva temple at Tungnath (3,680m), summit Chandrashila Peak for jaw-dropping 360° Himalayan views, and visit the mirror-like Deoria Tal lake. Every detail is handled — you just need to show up.
1

Delhi → Haridwar → Chopta

Departure · Overnight Drive · Mountain Arrival

  • Evening pickup from Delhi (7–9 PM)
  • Drive via Haridwar → Devprayag → Rudraprayag
  • Breakfast stop at Rudraprayag Sangam
  • Arrive Chopta by late morning (10–12 PM)
  • Camp / cottage check-in, freshen up & rest
  • Evening stroll through Chopta meadows, bonfire
2

Tungnath Temple + Chandrashila Peak

Highest Shiva Temple · 360° Himalayan Views

  • Early start (5 AM) for sunrise trek
  • Trek to Tungnath Temple (3,680m) — 3.5 km trail
  • Darshan at world's highest Shiva temple
  • Summit Chandrashila Peak (4,130m) — panoramic views
  • Views of Nanda Devi, Trishul, Kedar range
  • Descend by afternoon; rest & bonfire dinner
3

Deoria Tal + Ukhimath Village

Mirror Lake · Ancient Temple Town

  • Early breakfast; drive to Sari village
  • Trek to Deoria Tal (2,438m) — 2 km through oak forest
  • Crystal-clear lake reflecting Chaukhamba peaks
  • Photography session at the lake
  • Visit Ukhimath — ancient Kedarnath winter seat
  • Return to Chopta; stargazing night
4

Chopta → Delhi Departure

Check-out · Scenic Drive · Home

  • Early breakfast & camp check-out
  • Optional quick visit to Dugalbitta meadow
  • Drive back via Rudraprayag & Haridwar
  • Optional Haridwar Ganga Aarti stop (evening)
  • Drop back at Delhi by night (9–11 PM)
  • Memories of Himalayas packed, soul refreshed 🏔️

Frequently Asked Questions

Chopta is a small hamlet in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand, situated at an altitude of about 2,680 meters (8,800 ft). It lies in the Kedarnath Wildlife Sanctuary and serves as the base camp for the famous Tungnath and Chandrashila trek.
Chopta is approximately 450 km from Delhi by road. The journey takes around 10–12 hours. Most guests prefer an overnight departure from Delhi to arrive fresh in the morning.
By road: Delhi → Haridwar → Devprayag → Rudraprayag → Ukhimath → Chopta. Kafall provides private cab transfers from Delhi. By train: Take a train to Haridwar or Rishikesh, then a cab to Chopta (~200 km).
Chopta is beautiful year-round. Summer (March–June) offers green meadows and clear views. Monsoon (July–Sept) brings lush greenery. Winter (Nov–Feb) brings heavy snowfall — ideal for snow lovers. Tungnath Temple remains open from May to November.
Chopta is famous as the "Mini Switzerland of India" for its snow-covered meadows and rhododendron forests. It is the base for Tungnath — the world's highest Shiva temple — and Chandrashila Peak, offering panoramic views of Nanda Devi, Trishul, and the Kedarnath range.
Yes! The Tungnath trek (3.5 km) is moderate and suitable for beginners and older trekkers. Deoria Tal (2 km) is even easier and perfect for families with children. We guide every group according to their fitness level.
Trekking to Tungnath & Chandrashila, Deoria Tal lake visit, birdwatching (Monal pheasant, Himalayan Griffon), wildlife spotting in Kedarnath Sanctuary, photography, stargazing, bonfire evenings, and exploring Ukhimath village.
Yes! From December to March, Chopta receives heavy snowfall and the entire meadow turns into a snow-white wonderland. The trek to Tungnath in snow is one of the most magical experiences in Uttarakhand. Ice axes and crampons are provided on request.
Yes — from basic tent camps (₹1,500/night) to premium eco-resorts with Himalayan views (₹4,500+/night). Facilities are basic compared to developed hill stations, which is exactly what makes Chopta special. Kafall partners only with verified, quality-checked properties.
