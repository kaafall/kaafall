Day-by-Day Plan

2N 3D Mussoorie Itinerary

Your Mussoorie journey begins the moment we pick you up from Delhi. Over 3 days you'll drive through Dehradun to the pine-clad ridges of Mussoorie, explore colonial-era landmarks along Mall Road and Camel's Back Road, ride the ropeway up to Gun Hill for sweeping Himalayan views, cool off at the cascading Kempty Falls, and soak in the quiet charm of Landour's old cantonment lanes. Every detail is handled — you just need to show up.
1

Delhi → Dehradun → Mussoorie

Departure · Scenic Drive · Mountain Arrival

  • Early morning pickup from Delhi (5–7 AM)
  • Drive via Meerut / Roorkee → Dehradun
  • Breakfast stop en route
  • Arrive Mussoorie by afternoon (2–4 PM)
  • Hotel check-in, freshen up & rest
  • Evening stroll on Mall Road, local shopping
2

Gun Hill + Kempty Falls + Camel's Back Road

Ropeway Views · Waterfall · Sunset Walk

  • Post-breakfast ropeway ride to Gun Hill
  • Panoramic views of the snow-clad Himalayan range
  • Visit Kempty Falls — swim & photo stop
  • Lunch at a local Mussoorie eatery
  • Evening walk / pony ride on Camel's Back Road
  • Sunset views; return for bonfire dinner
3

Lal Tibba + Landour + Company Garden

Highest Point · Colonial Cantonment

  • Early breakfast; drive to Lal Tibba viewpoint
  • Telescope views of Bandarpunch & Himalayan peaks
  • Walk through Landour's old cantonment lanes
  • Visit Company Garden — boating & flower gardens
  • Café hop at Landour Bakehouse / Char Dukan
  • Check-out and free time for last-minute shopping
4

Mussoorie → Delhi Departure

Scenic Drive · Home

  • Check-out by noon after breakfast
  • Optional stop at Dehradun for Robber's Cave
  • Drive back via Roorkee / Meerut
  • Dinner stop en route (on request)
  • Drop back at Delhi by night (9–11 PM)
  • Memories of the hills packed, soul refreshed ⛰️

Frequently Asked Questions

Mussoorie is a popular hill station in the Dehradun district of Uttarakhand, situated at an altitude of about 2,005 meters (6,570 ft) in the foothills of the Garhwal Himalayas. It's known as the "Queen of the Hills."
Mussoorie is approximately 290 km from Delhi by road. The journey takes around 6–7 hours. Most guests prefer an early morning departure to arrive by afternoon.
By road: Delhi → Meerut/Roorkee → Dehradun → Mussoorie. Kafall provides private cab transfers from Delhi. By train: Take a train to Dehradun, then a cab to Mussoorie (~35 km, about 1.5 hours).
Mussoorie is pleasant year-round. Summer (March–June) offers cool weather, a welcome escape from the plains. Monsoon (July–Sept) brings lush greenery and misty views. Winter (Dec–Feb) occasionally sees snowfall — ideal for snow lovers.
Mussoorie is famous as the "Queen of the Hills" for its colonial architecture, Mall Road shopping, Kempty Falls, and Gun Hill ropeway views of the snow-clad Himalayan range. Landour, its quieter twin town, is loved for cafés and old cantonment charm.
Yes! Mussoorie is one of the most family and senior-friendly hill stations, with easy sightseeing, minimal trekking, ropeways, and good road connectivity. Ideal for a relaxed getaway with kids or elders.
Mall Road walks and shopping, Gun Hill ropeway ride, Kempty Falls visit, boating at Company Garden, cable car rides, café-hopping in Landour, photography at Lal Tibba, and short nature walks on Camel's Back Road.
Occasionally! Between late December and February, Mussoorie sometimes receives light to moderate snowfall, especially at higher points like Lal Tibba, turning the town into a scenic winter wonderland.
Yes — from budget hotels (₹1,800/night) to premium resorts with Himalayan views (₹6,500+/night). Mussoorie has excellent hospitality infrastructure being one of India's most established hill stations. Kafall partners only with verified, quality-checked properties.
Mall Road and Kempty Falls can get busy during summer and long weekends. However, areas like Landour and Camel's Back Road remain relatively peaceful even in peak season, offering a quieter alternative.
