Day-by-Day Plan

2N 3D Kanatal Itinerary

Your Kanatal journey begins the moment we pick you up from Delhi. A comfortable drive through Dehradun and Mussoorie brings you into Garhwal's quiet cedar country — a small hillside hamlet on the Mussoorie-Chamba road known for its dense deodar forests, camping meadows and views of Tehri Lake. Over the next two days you'll trek up to Surkanda Devi Temple, walk through silent pine and cedar woods, catch a glimpse of the Tehri Lake from a viewpoint, browse the Chamba market, and simply breathe the mountain quiet. Every detail is handled — you just need to show up.
1

Delhi → Kanatal

Departure · Scenic Drive · Arrival & Evening Bonfire

  • Early morning pickup from Delhi (5–6 AM)
  • Drive via Meerut → Roorkee → Dehradun → Mussoorie
  • Breakfast & lunch stops en route
  • Arrive Kanatal by evening; check-in to your camp/cottage
  • Evening stroll through the cedar & pine forest
  • Bonfire dinner under a clear mountain sky
2

Surkanda Devi & Tehri Lake View

Temple Trek · Dhanaulti · Deodar Forest Walk

  • Morning trek (~2 km) to Surkanda Devi Temple for panoramic Himalayan views
  • Drive to a Tehri Lake viewpoint for sweeping reservoir views
  • Visit Dhanaulti's Eco Park & deodar forest trail
  • Optional zip-line / valley crossing at the adventure camp
  • Afternoon free for a quiet cafe hop or rest
  • Evening bonfire and stargazing session
3

Chamba Market → Delhi Departure

Local Market · Check-out · Drive Home

  • Leisurely breakfast at the camp/cottage
  • Short stop at Chamba market for local woollens & pahadi snacks
  • Check-out and drive back via Mussoorie & Dehradun
  • Dinner stop en route (on request)
  • Drop back at Delhi by late evening, mind well and truly rested 🌲

Frequently Asked Questions

Kanatal is a small hill town in the Tehri Garhwal district of Uttarakhand, on the Mussoorie-Chamba road, at an altitude of about 2,135 meters (7,000 ft). It's known for its dense deodar cedar forests and camping culture.
Kanatal is approximately 330 km from Delhi by road. The journey takes around 8–9 hours via Meerut, Roorkee, Dehradun and Mussoorie — comfortably done in a single day.
By road: Delhi → Meerut → Roorkee → Dehradun → Mussoorie → Kanatal. Kafall provides private cab transfers directly from Delhi. By rail: Take a train to Dehradun, then a cab to Kanatal (~80 km).
March–June offers pleasant weather, perfect for camping and forest walks. September–November brings the clearest post-monsoon skies. December–February is chilly with regular snowfall, ideal for a cosy bonfire evening.
Kanatal is famous for its dense deodar and oak forests, camping and bonfire experiences, the Surkanda Devi Temple trek nearby, valley crossing / zip-line adventure activities, and its proximity to Tehri Lake and Mussoorie.
Yes — the forest walks and viewpoints are gentle, though the Surkanda Devi trek involves some stairs. Overall, Kanatal suits families, older travellers and anyone looking for a relaxed camping-style getaway.
Camping and bonfire evenings, the Surkanda Devi Temple trek, deodar forest walks, a Tehri Lake viewpoint visit, Dhanaulti's Eco Park, and zip-line / valley crossing adventure activities at select camps.
Yes, Kanatal receives regular snowfall in December–February and is one of the more reliable snow spots near Delhi, making it popular for winter camping trips.
Yes — from simple homestays (₹1,700/night) and riverside camps to premium boutique resorts with panoramic valley views (₹6,200+/night). Kafall partners only with verified, quality-checked properties.
Popular nearby spots include Surkanda Devi Temple (~10 km), Dhanaulti (~15 km), Chamba (~8 km), Tehri Lake (~35 km), Kaddukhal (~5 km) and Mussoorie (~40 km) — most are covered in our Standard and Premium packages.
