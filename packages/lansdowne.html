Day-by-Day Plan

2N 3D Lansdowne Itinerary

Your Lansdowne journey begins the moment we pick you up from Delhi. A comfortable drive through Moradabad, Kotdwar and the winding Garhwal foothills brings you into this quiet colonial cantonment town — home of the Garhwal Rifles, dense oak and pine forests, and a peaceful lake right in the middle of the market. Over the next two days you'll row across Bhulla Tal Lake, catch sweeping Himalayan views from Tip n Top, walk the old cantonment lanes past St. Mary's Church and the War Memorial, and simply breathe the forest quiet. Every detail is handled — you just need to show up.
1

Delhi → Lansdowne

Departure · Scenic Drive · Arrival & Evening Lake Walk

  • Early morning pickup from Delhi (5–6 AM)
  • Drive via Moradabad → Kotdwar → Dugadda
  • Breakfast & lunch stops en route
  • Arrive Lansdowne by evening; check-in to your cottage
  • Evening walk around Bhulla Tal Lake
  • Quiet dinner and rest amid the pine forest
2

Tip n Top & Heritage Walk

Tarkeshwar Mahadev · St. Mary's Church · War Memorial

  • Morning drive to Tip n Top viewpoint for panoramic Himalayan views
  • Visit Tarkeshwar Mahadev Temple set inside a deodar forest
  • Walk through the old cantonment past St. Mary's Church (now a war memorial museum)
  • Boating at Bhulla Tal Lake
  • Afternoon free for a quiet cafe hop or rest
  • Evening bonfire under a clear mountain sky
3

Local Market → Delhi Departure

Regimental Museum · Check-out · Drive Home

  • Leisurely breakfast at the cottage/resort
  • Quick visit to the Garhwal Rifles Regimental Museum (subject to timings)
  • Short stop at the local market for pahadi honey & handicrafts
  • Check-out and drive back via Kotdwar & Moradabad
  • Drop back at Delhi by late evening, mind well and truly rested 🌲

Frequently Asked Questions

Lansdowne is a small cantonment town in the Pauri Garhwal district of Uttarakhand, at an altitude of about 1,700 meters (5,600 ft). It's known for its colonial architecture, oak-pine forests and the Bhulla Tal Lake.
Lansdowne is approximately 260 km from Delhi by road. The journey takes around 6–7 hours via Moradabad and Kotdwar — one of the shortest Himalayan drives from Delhi.
By road: Delhi → Moradabad → Kotdwar → Dugadda → Lansdowne. Kafall provides private cab transfers directly from Delhi. By rail: Take a train to Kotdwar, then a cab to Lansdowne (~40 km).
March–June offers pleasant weather with clear forest walks. September–November brings the clearest post-monsoon skies and best Himalayan views. December–February is chilly with occasional light snowfall, ideal for a cosy cottage stay by the fireplace.
Lansdowne is famous for being the regimental centre of the Garhwal Rifles, its British-era colonial buildings, the Bhulla Tal Lake, the Tip n Top viewpoint, dense oak-pine forests, and its reputation as one of Uttarakhand's quietest, least commercialised hill towns.
Yes — Lansdowne's main attractions (lake, church, viewpoints, temple) involve gentle walks rather than strenuous treks, making it well suited for families, older travellers, and anyone looking for a relaxed hill getaway close to Delhi.
Boating at Bhulla Tal Lake, sunset/sunrise viewing at Tip n Top, visiting Tarkeshwar Mahadev Temple, exploring St. Mary's Church and the War Memorial, birdwatching in the forests, and quiet evenings by a bonfire.
Lansdowne occasionally receives light snowfall in January, though it's less frequent than higher hill stations like Kanatal — best treated as a bonus rather than a guarantee.
Yes — from simple homestays (₹1,600/night) to premium boutique resorts with panoramic Himalayan views (₹6,200+/night). Kafall partners only with verified, quality-checked properties.
Popular nearby spots include Bhulla Tal Lake and Tip n Top (right in/near town), Tarkeshwar Mahadev Temple (~13 km), St. Mary's Church & War Memorial (in town), Kotdwar (~40 km) and Jwalpa Devi Temple (~40 km) — most are covered in our Standard and Premium packages.
